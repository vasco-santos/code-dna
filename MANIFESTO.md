# The Code DNA Manifesto: The Rise of Resident Architects

Engineering is not just writing code; it is the preservation of intent, the management of complexity, and the continuous evolution of a system's DNA.

As AI agents transition from "guests" to active participants in our codebases, we must move beyond the era of **Passive Assistance** and enter the era of **Resident Architecture**.

## 1. Eliminate Instruction Drift
Instruction Drift is the entropy of engineering standards. When agents operate without a shared "Law of the Land," they introduce inconsistent patterns that dilute the codebase's integrity. **Code DNA ensures that every agent inherits the same foundational excellence.**

## 2. Standards are Living DNA
Standards should not live in static wikis where they go to die. They must be **Injected**, **Symlinked**, and **Enforced**. A standard is only real if it is present in the agent's immediate context at the moment of creation.

## 3. Plan-First, Code-Second
Quality is born in the strategy phase, not the execution phase. Agents must be mandated to **Research**, **Plan**, and **Seek Feedback** before a single line of code is committed. We value the "Why" as much as the "What."

## 4. Architectural Auditability
Every change is a session. Every decision is an ADR. We preserve the evolution of thought through **DNA Sessions**, ensuring that the rationale behind every architectural choice is versioned and permanent.

## 5. From Guests to Resident Architects
An AI agent should not need to be told how we name variables or structure services every time it starts. It should arrive with that knowledge pre-encoded. Code DNA transforms agents into **Resident Architects** who know the house as well as the builder.

---

### **Questions for the Future of Code DNA**
To refine this manifesto and our roadmap, let's discuss:
1. **The Human-AI Contract**: Should the "Approval" step be strictly human, or can we envision a "Lead Architect Agent" that enforces the DNA of other agents?
2. **DNA Evolution**: How should the system handle "Mutations"—when an agent discovers a better pattern than the one currently in the Global DNA?
3. **Multi-Repo Governance**: In a world of hundreds of micro-services, how do we prevent "DNA Divergence" across different teams?
